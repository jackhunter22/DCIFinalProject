How to Play

Disc Golf is similar to Golf in that the gist of the game is to have as few shots as possible through 18 holes. Each hole is a unique challenge that a player tries to conquer in as few strokes as possible. In Disc Golf, however, players throw golf discs until they can throw it into a metal basket, with chains to catch the disc.

Golf discs are different than ultimate frisbees. They are smaller and often lighter, but the biggest difference is a sharper edge, which makes golf discs much more aerodynamic. You should not try and play catch with them. Players use three types of discs: drivers, mid-ranges, and putters.

Putters have the rounder edges, making them slower and more controllable. They are good for approach shots and putting. Mid-ranges have a sharper edge than putters but still have a rounder edge than drivers. They will turn and fade more but will also fly faster if given more force. Drivers have sharp edges that cut through the air to allow players to throw long shots. These discs are harder to control and can fade hard if not thrown hard enough.

Each disc has unique characteristics that make them valuable. Players care about how fast the disc will fly, which direction will it go (left/right), and whether it will glide in the air or push itself down.
